Left-Authoritarian

Left-Authoritarian politicians are those who believe in a strong government that controls the economy and society. They are in favor of social programs and regulations that protect the environment and workers.

Right-Authoritarian

Right-Authoritarian politicians believe in a strong, centralized government that enforces strict laws and regulations to maintain order and control. They often support traditional values and may prioritize national security and economic stability over individual freedoms.

Right-Libertarian

Right-Libertarian politicians advocate for minimal government intervention in both personal and economic matters. They believe in individual liberty, free-market capitalism, and limited government. They often support lower taxes, deregulation, and privatization of public services.

Left-Libertarian

Left-Libertarian politicians emphasize social equality and individual freedom. They advocate for a decentralized government and believe in reducing the power of large corporations and the state. They support social justice, environmental sustainability, and workers' rights.
